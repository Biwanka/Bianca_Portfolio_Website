Contained Contamination: Crystallizing the Abject

3D

Contained Contamination explores the transformation of perceived bodily pollution/corruption into beauty through procedural entanglement. The work is inspired by my visibly patterned veins, which become more prominent with temperature changes. In cold conditions, my red veins become prominent and in warm conditions, my blue veins. I have experienced different situations growing up that have been a blow to my confidence. In primary school, a classmate once asked if it was contagious. Another situation is in high school, where after I got hit in the face with a racket in gym, as I iced my face, the teacher mistook the natural prominence of my red vein patterns as my condition getting worse. These moments framed my body as diseased or polluted.

This project reclaims that narrative. I created three iterations of a marble naked female torso resembling a jewelry display mannequin (headless, armless, and cropped at the waist). This choice was to desexualize the form and shift focus from the nude body to surface, to perception. Across the iterations, the veins evolve from organic emission and invasive contamination into crystallized adornment, reframing what was once perceived as abject into something precious and curated.
